Nina Simone

High Priestess of Soul

Timeline of Nina Simones´s life

  • 1933 Nina Simone is born Eunice Kathleen Waymon in Tryon, North Carolina on February 21, 1933.
  • 1950 Simone graduates from Allen High School for Girls in Asheville, North Carolina. She spends the summer at the Juilliard School as a student of Carl Friedberg, preparing for an audition at the Curtis Institute of Music in Philadelphia. Her application was later denied, which Simone believed was due to racial discrimination.
  • 1955 Simone's first public performance under the name "Nina Simone" at the Midtown Bar and Grill in Atlantic City.
  • 1957 Simone records her first album, "Little Girl Blue," a mixture of jazz, blues and classical.
  • 1959 "Little Girl Blue" is released. After the album's success, Simone signs a contract with Colpix Records and records a number of studio and live albums, including live albums "Nina Simone at Town Hall" and "The Amazing Nina Simone."
  • 1960s Simone’s music and voice become intertwined with the civil rights movement.
  • 1960 Release of her album, "Nina at Newport."
  • 1961 Release of her studio album, "Forbidden Fruit," Simone's fifth album since "Little Girl Blue."
  • 1962 Release of albums "At the Village Gate" and "Nina Simone Sings Ellington."
  • 1963 “Mississippi Goddamn” is written and released in response to the assassination of Medgar Evers and the bombing of the 16th Street Baptist Church in Birmingham, Alabama.
  • 1964 Simone signs to Philips Records, a Dutch company that allows her to record songs that draw on her African American heritage. Release of her studio album, "Broadway - Blues - Ballads."
  • 1965 Release of her studio albums, "Let It All Out," "I Put a Spell on You" and "Pastel Blues."
  • 1966 Release of her studio album, "Wild Is the Wind."
  • 1967 Release of her studio albums, "High Priestess of Soul," "Nina Simone Sings the Blues" and "Silk & Soul."
  • 1968 Release of her studio and live album, "'Nuff Said!"
  • 1969 Release of her studio album, "Black Gold."
  • 1970 Release of her studio album, "Gifted & Black."
  • 1971 Release of her 13th studio album, "Here Comes the Sun."
  • 1973 Simone leaves the United States for Barbados. She relocates to Liberia, then to various places in Europe, where she spends the rest of her life.
  • 1978 Release of her 14th studio album, "Baltimore."
  • 1993 Release of her final album, "A Single Woman."
  • APRIL 21, 2003 Nina Simone dies at her home in Carry-le-Rouet, France at the age of 70.
  • 2018 Simone's childhood home in Tryon, North Carolina is designated a National Treasure by the National Trust for Historic Preservation.
